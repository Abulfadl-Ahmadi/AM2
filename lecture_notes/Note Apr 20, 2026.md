## 2. 1D Elastic Collision Review (برخورد کشسان یک بعدی)

**Original Notes Transcription:**
> **■ برخورد کشسان:** (Elastic Collision)
> - برخورد کشسان در دو بعد (Elastic collision in 2D)
> - سطح مقطع برخورد (Scattering cross section)
> 
> **1-d:**
> $$v'_1 = -v_1 + 2V_{CM}$$
> $$v'_2 = -v_2 + 2V_{CM}$$
> 
> Lab: $v$ (before), $v'$ (after)
> CM: $u$ (before), $u'$ (after)
> 
> Projectile/Bullet ($1$ at velocity $v_1$) colliding with stationary Target ($2$ at rest, $v_2 = 0$):
> $$V_{CM} = \frac{m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2} \left[ + \frac{m_2 v_2}{m_1 + m_2} \right]$$
> $$\rightsquigarrow v'_1 = -v_1 + \frac{2m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2} = \frac{-m_1 v_1 - m_2 v_1 + 2m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2} = \frac{(m_1 - m_2) v_1}{m_1 + m_2}$$
> 
> **• حالت‌های مختلف جواب بالا:** (Cases of the above result)
> - $v'_1 > 0 \quad (m_1 > m_2)$ : Projectile continues moving forward.
> - $v'_1 < 0 \quad (m_1 < m_2)$ : Projectile bounces backward.
> - $v'_1 = 0 \quad (m_1 = m_2)$ : Projectile comes to a complete stop, transferring all momentum.

> **Elaboration:**
> In a one-dimensional elastic collision where target particle 2 is initially at rest ($v_2 = 0$), the final velocity of projectile particle 1 in the lab frame simplifies to:
> $$v'_1 = \frac{m_1 - m_2}{m_1 + m_2} v_1$$
> 
> The physical behavior depends entirely on the relative masses of the two particles:
> - **Heavy Projectile ($m_1 > m_2$):** The numerator is positive, meaning the projectile keeps moving forward in its original direction after the collision.
> - **Light Projectile ($m_1 < m_2$):** The numerator is negative, meaning the projectile bounces back in the opposite direction.
> - **Equal Masses ($m_1 = m_2$):** The final velocity is zero. The projectile stops completely, transferring all of its kinetic energy and momentum to the target particle.

## 3. 2D Elastic Collision Kinematics (برخورد کشسان دو بعدی)

**Original Notes Transcription:**
> **■ برخورد در دو بعد:** (Collision in 2D)
> Find $\cos\phi = ?$ (or scattering angles in Lab frame)
> 
> Lab frame diagram:
> Bullet (1) with velocity $\vec{v}_1$ moves towards stationary Target (2) at rest ($v_2 = 0$).
> *همواره دستگاهی داریم که در آن $v_2 = 0$ باشد.* (We can always choose a frame where $v_2 = 0$).
> After collision: particle 1 scatters at angle $\psi$, particle 2 scatters at angle $\xi$.
> 
> **Step I) مهاجرت به CM** (Migration to CM Frame):
> $$\vec{V}_{CM} = \frac{m_1 \vec{v}_1}{m_1 + m_2}$$
> $$\rightarrow \vec{u}_1 = \vec{v}_1 - \vec{V}_{CM} = \vec{v}_1 - \frac{m_1}{m_1 + m_2} \vec{v}_1 \rightarrow \boxed{\vec{u}_1 = \frac{m_2}{m_1 + m_2} \vec{v}_1}$$
> $$\rightarrow \boxed{\vec{u}_2 = -\frac{m_1}{m_1 + m_2} \vec{v}_1}$$
> (Note: $m_1 \vec{u}_1 + m_2 \vec{u}_2 = 0$)
> 
> **Step II) برخورد کشسان:** (Elastic Collision in CM)
> $$\frac{1}{2} m_1 u_1^2 + \frac{1}{2} m_2 u_2^2 = \frac{1}{2} m_1 {u'_1}^2 + \frac{1}{2} m_2 {u'_2}^2$$
> **قانون مقدس (پایستگی تکانه خطی):** (Conservation of Linear Momentum)
> $$m_1 \vec{u}_1 + m_2 \vec{u}_2 = m_1 \vec{u}'_1 + m_2 \vec{u}'_2 = 0 \quad (\text{CM-frame})$$
> 
> **Step III) جواب معادله (دسته جواب):** (Solution set)
> $$\begin{cases} |\vec{u}'_1| = |\vec{u}_1| \\ |\vec{u}'_2| = |\vec{u}_2| \end{cases}$$

> **Elaboration:**
> To analyze a two-dimensional elastic collision, we begin in the Lab frame where target particle 2 is at rest. We then shift to the Center of Mass (CM) frame:
> 1. The CM velocity vector is $\vec{V}_{CM} = \frac{m_1}{m_1+m_2} \vec{v}_1$.
> 2. The initial velocities in the CM frame are $\vec{u}_1 = \frac{m_2}{m_1+m_2} \vec{v}_1$ and $\vec{u}_2 = -\frac{m_1}{m_1+m_2} \vec{v}_1$.
> 
> In the CM frame, the total momentum is zero by definition, so the particles must move in opposite directions both before and after the collision. Applying conservation of momentum and conservation of kinetic energy reveals that the magnitudes of the velocities do not change during the collision: $|\vec{u}'_1| = |\vec{u}_1|$ and $|\vec{u}'_2| = |\vec{u}_2|$. 
> 
> Therefore, in the CM frame, the collision only rotates the velocity vectors by a scattering angle $\theta$ relative to the initial axis of motion.

## 4. Transforming Scattering Angles to the Lab Frame (تبدیل زوایای پراکندگی به دستگاه آزمایشگاه)

**Original Notes Transcription:**
> *- حال فرض می‌کنیم $\theta$ را داریم، زاویه‌ی پراکندگی در دستگاه آزمایشگاه (ها) را بیابید:*
> (Assuming we have the CM angle $\theta$, find the Lab frame angles $\psi$ and $\xi$):
> 
> In Lab frame:
> $$\text{(I) } \begin{cases} \vec{v}'_1 = \vec{u}'_1 + \vec{V}_{CM} \\ \vec{v}'_2 = \vec{u}'_2 + \vec{V}_{CM} \end{cases} \qquad \text{(II) } \begin{cases} |\vec{u}'_1| = |\vec{u}_1| \\ |\vec{u}'_2| = |\vec{u}_2| \end{cases}$$
> 
> In the CM diagram:
> $\vec{u}'_1$ has components $(u_1 \cos\theta, u_1 \sin\theta)$ relative to the horizontal.
> $\vec{v}'_1 = (u_1 \cos\theta + V_{CM}, u_1 \sin\theta)$
> 
> $$\rightarrow \tan\psi = \frac{u_1 \sin\theta}{u_1 \cos\theta + V_{CM}} = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta + \frac{V_{CM}}{u_1}}$$
> 
> Since:
> $$|\vec{u}_1| = \frac{m_2}{m_1+m_2} |\vec{v}_1| \qquad |\vec{V}_{CM}| = \frac{m_1}{m_1+m_2} |\vec{v}_1| \rightarrow \frac{V_{CM}}{u_1} = \frac{m_1}{m_2}$$
> 
> $$\rightarrow \boxed{\tan\psi = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta + \frac{m_1}{m_2}}}$$
> 
> **For the target angle $\xi$:**
> $$\tan\xi = \frac{u_2 \sin\theta}{V_{CM} - u_2 \cos\theta} = \frac{\sin\theta}{\frac{V_{CM}}{u_2} - \cos\theta}$$
> Since $|\vec{u}_2| = \frac{m_1}{m_1+m_2} |\vec{v}_1|$ and $|\vec{V}_{CM}| = \frac{m_1}{m_1+m_2} |\vec{v}_1| \rightarrow \frac{V_{CM}}{u_2} = 1$
> 
> $$\rightarrow \tan\xi = \frac{\sin\theta}{1 - \cos\theta} = \frac{2\sin(\theta/2)\cos(\theta/2)}{2\sin^2(\theta/2)} = \cot(\theta/2) \rightarrow \boxed{\tan\xi = \cot(\theta/2)}$$

> **Elaboration:**
> The scattering angle in the Lab frame ($\psi$ for the projectile, $\xi$ for the target) can be found by adding the Galilean boost vector $\vec{V}_{CM}$ to the CM velocity vectors:
> 
> 1. **For the Projectile ($\psi$):** The horizontal component of the final velocity is $u_1 \cos\theta + V_{CM}$, and the vertical component is $u_1 \sin\theta$. Taking the ratio gives the tangent of the Lab angle $\psi$. Substituting the ratio $\frac{V_{CM}}{u_1} = \frac{m_1}{m_2}$ yields the relation:
>    $$\tan\psi = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta + \frac{m_1}{m_2}}$$
> 2. **For the Target ($\xi$):** Because the target particle moves in the opposite direction in the CM frame, its horizontal component is $V_{CM} - u_2 \cos\theta$ and its vertical component is $u_2 \sin\theta$. Substituting $\frac{V_{CM}}{u_2} = 1$ simplifies the tangent of its deflection angle to:
>    $$\tan\xi = \frac{\sin\theta}{1-\cos\theta} = \cot\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)$$
>    This means that in the Lab frame, the target particle always scatters at a simple angle of $\xi = \frac{\pi - \theta}{2}$ regardless of the masses.

## 5. Limiting and Special Cases (حالت‌های خاص و حدی)

**Original Notes Transcription:**
> **حالت‌های حدی:** (Limiting Cases)
> 
> **I) $m_1 \ll m_2$:**
> $$\psi \approx \theta$$
> 
> **II) $m_1 = m_2$ (billiard):**
> $$\tan\psi = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta + 1} = \frac{2\sin(\theta/2)\cos(\theta/2)}{2\cos^2(\theta/2)} = \tan(\theta/2) \rightarrow \boxed{\psi = \theta/2}$$
> 
> **⚠️ حداکثر اندازه زاویه $\theta$ چقدر است؟**
> $$\theta_{max} = \pi$$
> - اگر $\theta_{max} = \pi$ در حالت $m_1 = m_2$، حداکثر زاویه انحراف $\psi_{max} = \pi/2$ است. (Physicist)
> 
> **For $m_1 = m_2$:**
> $$\begin{cases} \tan\psi = \tan(\theta/2) \\ \tan\xi = \cot(\theta/2) \end{cases} \rightarrow \tan\psi \times \tan\xi = 1 \rightarrow \boxed{\psi + \xi = \pi/2}$$

> **Elaboration:**
> We analyze several key limiting cases for the projectile scattering angle $\psi$:
> - **Light Projectile ($m_1 \ll m_2$):** When the projectile is extremely light compared to the target (such as an electron scattering off an atomic nucleus), the ratio $\frac{m_1}{m_2} \rightarrow 0$. The formula reduces to $\tan\psi \approx \tan\theta$, meaning the Lab frame scattering angle is approximately equal to the CM frame scattering angle.
> - **Equal Masses ($m_1 = m_2$):** For particles of equal mass (such as two billiard balls), the formula simplifies using trigonometric identities to $\psi = \frac{\theta}{2}$. 
>   - Since the maximum value of the CM scattering angle is $\theta_{max} = \pi$ (corresponding to a head-on backscattering event), the maximum deflection angle of the projectile in the Lab frame is exactly $\psi_{max} = \frac{\pi}{2} = 90^\circ$.
>   - Additionally, since $\tan\psi = \tan(\theta/2)$ and $\tan\xi = \cot(\theta/2)$, we have $\tan\psi \cdot \tan\xi = 1$. This mathematical identity proves that $\psi + \xi = 90^\circ$. In other words, after an elastic collision between two equal-mass particles (one initially at rest), the two particles always scatter at right angles to each other.

## 6. Graphical Interpretation and Restricted Deflection (تفسیر نموداری و انحراف محدود)

**Original Notes Transcription:**
> **Step IV) بررسی زوایای پراکندگی: به حالت‌های خاص به بررسی $\psi$ می‌پردازیم:**
> $$\tan\psi = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta + \frac{m_1}{m_2}} \qquad \theta < \pi$$
> 
> **• (A) $\frac{m_1}{m_2} \le 1$:**
> $$-\cos\theta_* = \frac{m_1}{m_2} \rightsquigarrow \tan\psi = \infty \rightarrow \psi = \pi/2$$
> 
> **Case I) $\frac{m_1}{m_2} < 1 \rightarrow \frac{|\vec{V}_{CM}|}{|\vec{u}_1|} < 1$:**
> • توصیف نموداری (Graphical Description):
> The Lab origin lies *inside* the velocity circle of radius $|\vec{u}_1|$.
> $$\psi_{max} = \pi \quad \left(\text{for } \frac{m_1}{m_2} < 1\right)$$
> 
> **Case II) $\frac{m_1}{m_2} > 1 \rightarrow \frac{|\vec{V}_{CM}|}{|\vec{u}_1|} > 1$:**
> Graphical Description:
> The Lab origin lies *outside* the velocity circle of radius $|\vec{u}_1|$.
> The maximum Lab deflection angle $\psi_{max}$ occurs when the final velocity vector is tangent to the circle:
> $$\sin\psi_{max} = \frac{|\vec{u}_1|}{|\vec{V}_{CM}|} = \frac{m_2}{m_1} \rightarrow \boxed{\psi_{max} = \arcsin\left(\frac{m_2}{m_1}\right)}$$

> **Elaboration:**
> A graphical construction (using velocity circles) provides a clear geometric understanding of scattering:
> 
> 1. **Light Projectile ($m_1 < m_2$):** In this case, $V_{CM} < u_1$. Geometrically, the Lab origin lies inside the circle of possible CM velocity vectors $\vec{u}'_1$. Because the origin is inside, the vector sum $\vec{v}'_1 = \vec{V}_{CM} + \vec{u}'_1$ can point in any direction from $0$ to $\pi$. Thus, the projectile can scatter at any angle, including backwards ($\psi_{max} = \pi$).
> 2. **Heavy Projectile ($m_1 > m_2$):** In this case, $V_{CM} > u_1$. Geometrically, the Lab origin lies outside the circle of radius $u_1$. The final velocity vector in the Lab frame, $\vec{v}'_1$, is constrained to a cone of directions. The maximum deflection angle $\psi_{max}$ is reached when $\vec{v}'_1$ is tangent to the velocity circle. At this tangent point, $\vec{v}'_1$ and $\vec{u}'_1$ form a right triangle with the CM velocity vector $\vec{V}_{CM}$ as the hypotenuse. 
> 
> The maximum angle is therefore given by:
> $$\sin\psi_{max} = \frac{u_1}{V_{CM}} = \frac{m_2}{m_1}$$
> For example, if $m_1$ is twice the mass of $m_2$, the maximum scattering angle in the Lab frame is restricted to $\psi_{max} = \arcsin(0.5) = 30^\circ$.
