# Exam Problem Solver Workflow

Use this workflow to solve problems and answer questions related to the "AM II" (Applied Mathematics II) midterm exam.

## Step 1: Analyze the Problem
1. Read the user's question or problem description carefully.
2. **Trap & Trick Detection:** Analyze whether the question contains subtle details, edge cases, or "tricks" intentionally designed to mislead students. If you spot a trick, make a note to explicitly point it out in your response (e.g., "Note: Be careful here because...").
3. Identify exactly what is being asked and what mathematical tools are required.

## Step 2: Search the Knowledge Base
1. Use file search tools to look up relevant material in the `lecture_notes/` directory.
2. Search for related concepts, formulas, and examples in the `textbook/` directory.
3. **Crucial Rule:** Your answers must prioritize and be based upon these specific materials. Do not rely solely on general knowledge if specific methods are taught in the course.

## Step 3: Draft the Solution
Write your response following these exam-quality guidelines:
1. **Exam-Quality Standard:** Aim for full marks (100%). Be highly accurate, fully detailed, well-structured, and comprehensive. No brief or superficial answers.
2. **Step-by-Step Analysis:** Explain every step transparently and logically. Clearly state the formulas, theorems, and assumptions being used.
3. **Integrating Sources:** Explicitly mention how the professor's approach (from `lecture_notes`) compares or combines with the explanations in the `textbook` when applicable.
4. **Formatting & Language:** Use clear, academic English and proper mathematical/engineering terminology. Use markdown math rendering where appropriate.

## Step 4: Final Review
1. Review your drafted solution against the original question. Did you miss any points?
2. Verify that the answer aligns perfectly with the professor's notes and the textbook.
3. Ensure no steps were skipped and the logic is flawless.
4. Present the final answer to the user.
